Case Study: Redesign at Hustle

2019

Context

After a frenetic 2018 spent adding new features, we took stock of the product in 2019 and saw that scaling had degraded our core navigation patterns. We redesigned the app, focusing on updating outdated brand colors and creating a header navigation pattern that accomodated four levels of page hierarchy.

Role

I worked with another designer on the team to set the vision and direction for the redesign, then worked as the PM on the project, manging the engineering and internal and external rollout.

Work

The state of the product before this redesign, with outdated and unattractive colors, and a poor use of space.
We identified that the primary issue was our navigation patterns. Here, a critical page (stats on an SMS campaign) has key information buried underneath multiple tiers of navigation, each designed independently and without holistic hierarchy. Also pictured here is the sidebar, which showed other active groups in an organization, but was unimportant for doing work within a group.
We started by auditing all the pages in the product, and mapping the relationship and hiearchy between them.
Using this map, we built a header that used a consistent pattern of breadcrumbs, title, and tabs that accomodated pages at all layers of the product's hierarchy. Here are examples for the four differnet types of pages we found.
We moved the list of groups to a sidebar menu that expands on hover, giving users the same level of quick access without the visual distraction of permanent visibility.
The launched verison of the product incorporated the header and sidebar changes, and updated the colors and iconography.

Results

This project completed on schedule, and was launched to universal appreciation from our internal stakeholders (especially Sales), and our clients.
